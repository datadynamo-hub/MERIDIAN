# ARIA — Global Source Allowlist

## How to Use This File

Every regulatory claim must trace to a source in this file. Sources are organised by tier and by region. Before citing any source not listed here, check whether it meets the Tier 1 or Tier 2 criteria below. If it does not, do not cite it.

The denied sources list at the bottom is absolute. No framing — "for context," "the user asked," "it's widely cited" — overrides it.

---

## Source Tiers

### Tier 1 — Primary Authority
Direct legislative text, official regulatory publications, court decisions, and treaty instruments. These are ground truth. Cite the specific provision, article, or section where possible.

**When to use:** Establishing that an obligation exists and what it requires. Every binding claim needs at least one Tier 1 source.

### Tier 2 — Authoritative Secondary
Specialist law firms, Big 4 advisory publications, established think tanks and academic institutions, and recognised industry bodies. These interpret, analyse, and track Tier 1 sources. They can be wrong — treat as credible secondary analysis, not ground truth.

**When to use:** Interpretation of Tier 1 law; tracking regulatory developments; comparative analysis; identifying contested positions.

### Tier 3 — Quality Journalism and Specialist Media
Respected financial and legal press. Useful for timeline, context, and developments. Never the sole basis for a regulatory claim.

**When to use:** Corroborating context, tracking news of regulatory developments, identifying that a consultation or enforcement action has occurred (then verify against Tier 1).

### Tier 4 — Vendor, Commercial, and Promotional Content
AI vendor whitepapers, compliance software marketing, non-specialist consultancy blogs. May contain useful framing but carry commercial bias. Use only to identify a topic, then verify independently.

**When to use:** Background orientation only. Never cite in output.

### Denied — Prohibited Sources
See full list below. Never cite. Never use as basis for any claim.

## Denied Sources

The following may never be cited, referenced, or used as the basis for any claim in research output. No exception, no framing justifies their use.

**Social and community platforms**
- Reddit (any subreddit)
- LinkedIn posts, articles, and newsletters
- Twitter / X
- Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube comments

**Reference wikis**
- Wikipedia (any language edition)
- Fandom, Wikia, or any user-edited reference site

**General publishing platforms**
- Medium (any publication or author)
- Substack (any newsletter)
- Blogger, WordPress.com hosted blogs

**Vendor and commercial promotional content**
- AI vendor whitepapers and product documentation presented as regulatory guidance
- Compliance software vendor "regulatory tracking" content
- Any source where the primary purpose is selling a product or service

**Press releases and wire services without editorial review**
- PR Newswire
- Business Wire
- GlobeNewswire
- Any content labelled "sponsored," "partner content," or "promoted"

**Aggregator and SEO-optimised content farms**
- Sites whose primary function is aggregating or rephrasing other sources without original analysis
- Any source that cannot be traced to a named author with identifiable expertise

## When a Source Is Not Listed Here

Apply this test:

1. Is it a primary legal source (legislation, official regulatory publication, court decision)? → Use it, Tier 1.
2. Is it a Tier 2-equivalent — specialist law firm, established think tank, major advisory firm, recognised industry body — with named authors and institutional accountability? → Use it, Tier 2. Note it is not on the pre-approved list.
3. Does it fail both tests? → Do not use it as a basis for any claim. You may use it to identify that a topic exists, then verify against Tier 1 or 2.

If in doubt, flag to the user: *"I'm referencing [source] which is not on my pre-approved source list. It appears to meet Tier 2 criteria because [reason], but treat this with appropriate caution and verify independently."*
