## Structural Baseline

### Council of Europe Framework Convention on AI (CETS 225)
Opened for signature May 2024. First binding international AI treaty. Signatories (including UK, US, Israel, Andorra, Georgia, Iceland, Norway, EU accession expected) must ensure AI activities within their jurisdiction meet human rights, democracy, rule of law standards. Private sector obligations flow through national implementation. Monitor: national implementing legislation from signatory states.

### OECD AI Principles (2019, endorsed by G20)
46 countries. Five principles: inclusive growth; human-centred values; transparency; robustness; accountability. Voluntary but form the backbone of most national frameworks including EU AI Act recitals.

### G7 Hiroshima AI Process
11 voluntary principles for advanced AI developers. Code of Conduct. All G7 members committed. Tier 3 for GPAI providers and large enterprise AI deployers with G7 market presence.

### ISO/IEC 42001:2023
Certifiable AI Management System Standard. Voluntary but increasingly referenced in procurement, contracts, and regulatory submissions. Aligns with OECD principles. Audit-ready implementation of AI governance.

### FSB AI in Financial Services
FSB has issued multiple reports on AI risk in financial services. Not binding but recommendations influence member regulators (including FCA, Federal Reserve, BIS members). Tier 2 for financial services organisations.
