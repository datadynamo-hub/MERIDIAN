## Structural Baseline

### Expected regulatory landscape
No binding cross-sector AI law. Sector regulators apply existing powers to AI:
- FCA: Consumer Duty (outcomes for retail customers); model risk (SS1/23 via PRA but FCA-aligned); AI in regulated activities subject to Principle 6 (customers' interests) and Principle 12 (consumer duty)
- ICO: UK GDPR Art.22 (automated decisions with legal/significant effect — right to explanation, human review, opt-out); accountability obligations; DPIA where high risk
- PRA: SS1/23 model risk management — governance, validation, independent review for models used in regulatory capital and risk decisions

### Expected pending developments
- UK AI Bill — government has signalled sector-led approach but statutory duty of care remains possible. No confirmed parliamentary timetable as of May 2025.
- AI Safety Institute — advisory and evaluation function, not regulatory. Monitor for any mandate expansion.

### Expected penalties
ICO: Up to £17.5M or 4% global turnover for UK GDPR violations. FCA: Unlimited financial penalties; business restriction; withdrawal of authorisation.
